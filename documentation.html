Risk Disclosure Statement for Investments
This Risk Disclosure Statement serves to inform you about the inherent risks associated with investing in financial markets. Before making any investment decisions, it's important to understand these risks and consider your individual circumstances.
Market Risk
Investments in financial markets can rise or fall in value due to market fluctuations, economic conditions, or changes in interest rates. Past performance is not indicative of future results.
Credit Risk
The issuer of a security may default on its obligations, leading to potential losses. This risk is particularly significant with bonds and fixed-income securities.
Liquidity Risk
Some investments may not be easily sellable. In the case of illiquid investments, you may have to hold them for longer periods or potentially incur losses when trying to sell.
Investment Strategy Risk
The success of investment strategies may vary, and no strategy guarantees profit. A strategy that performed well in the past may not perform well in the future.
Country and Currency Risk
Investments in foreign securities may be subject to risks associated with political instability, economic changes, and currency fluctuations, which can affect returns.
Operational Risk
Issues related to systems, processes, and personnel may disrupt the normal functioning of investment services, potentially impacting performance.
Regulatory Risk
Changes in laws or regulations can adversely affect the value of investments and alter the conditions under which businesses operate.
Tax Implications
Investments may have different tax consequences, which can significantly impact overall returns. Consult a tax professional for personalized advice.
Volatility
Certain investments can experience considerable price swings, leading to significant gains or losses over short periods.
Investors should conduct thorough research or consult with a qualified financial advisor to ensure they fully understand the risks involved before making investment decisions. By investing, you acknowledge and accept these risks.

Internal Controls and Risk Management Framework Policy
Purpose
The purpose of this Internal Controls and Risk Management Framework Policy is to establish a structured approach to identifying, assessing, monitoring, and managing risks within the organization. This policy aims to safeguard assets, ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations, enhance operational efficiency, and promote transparency and accountability in all business activities.

Scope
This policy applies to all employees, management, and stakeholders within the organization and covers all areas of operations, including finance, investments, compliance, and information technology.
Definitions
- Internal Controls: The processes put in place to ensure the integrity of financial and accounting information, promote accountability and prevent fraud.
- Risk Management: The identification, assessment, and prioritization of risks followed by coordinated efforts to minimize, monitor, and control the probability or impact of unfortunate events.
Objectives
- To promote a risk-aware culture within the organization. - To establish clear guidelines for identifying, assessing, and managing risks. - To ensure compliance with laws, regulations, and industry standards. - To safeguard organizational assets and resources. - To enhance decision-making and operational efficiency.
Governance Structure - Board of Directors: The Board is responsible for overseeing the organization’s risk management framework and ensuring that it is aligned with the overall business strategy.
- Risk Management Committee: Composed of key management personnel, this committee is responsible for implementing the risk management framework and reporting on risk exposure and mitigation strategies.
- Internal Audit: The Internal Audit function will assess the effectiveness of internal controls and compliance with established policies and procedures.
Risk Assessment Process
- Risk Identification: Regularly identify and document potential risks that could affect the achievement of organizational objectives. This should include both external and internal risks.
- Risk Analysis: Assess the likelihood and impact of identified risks using established criteria to prioritize them effectively. This analysis should be qualitative and quantitative where applicable.
- Risk Evaluation: Determine whether risks are acceptable based on the organization’s risk appetite and tolerance levels.
Control Activities
- Control Environment: Foster an ethical culture and ensure that management demonstrates commitment to effective internal controls and risk management.
- Preventive Controls: Implement measures to prevent the occurrence of risks, such as segregation of duties, approval processes, and access controls.
- Detective Controls: Establish mechanisms to detect and respond to risks that materialize, such as reconciliations, audits, and monitoring activities.
- Corrective Controls: Develop procedures for addressing issues and weaknesses identified, ensuring prompt remedial actions are taken.
Monitoring and Review
- Ongoing Monitoring: Regularly review and monitor risk management and control activities to ensure they are functioning effectively and adjust as needed.
- Periodic Review: Conduct comprehensive reviews of the risk management framework and internal controls at least annually or following significant changes in operations or regulatory requirements.
Training and Awareness
We will provide training for employees at all levels to ensure they understand the importance of internal controls and risk management and their individual roles in implementing these practices.
Reporting and Communication
Establish clear communication channels to report risks and control deficiencies. Any significant risk, control breakdown, or fraud should be reported immediately to senior management and the Board.
Documentation and Record Keeping
Maintain detailed records of risk assessments, control activities, monitoring results, and action plans to support accountability and continuous improvement.
Compliance with Laws and Regulations
Ensure that all internal controls and risk management practices comply with applicable laws, regulations, and industry standards.
This Internal Controls and Risk Management Framework Policy is designed to integrate risk management into the governance framework of our organization, strengthening our capacity to achieve strategic objectives while safeguarding assets and enhancing stakeholder confidence.

Loan Agreement Policy Purpose
The purpose of this Loan Agreement Policy is to establish clear guidelines and procedures for the issuance, management, and repayment of loans within the organization. The policy aims to ensure transparency, accountability, and compliance with all applicable laws and regulations while safeguarding the financial interests of the organization.

Scope
This policy applies to all departments and employees involved in the process of granting, managing, and collecting loans administered by the organization. It encompasses all types of loans, including personal loans, business loans, and any other lending products provided by the organization.
Definitions
- Loan: Any sum of money lent to a borrower with the expectation that it will be repaid, usually with interest, within an agreed-upon time frame.
- Borrower: An individual or entity that receives funds from the organization under the terms of a loan agreement.
- Lender: The organization providing the loan to the borrower.
Loan Types
The organization may offer various types of loans, which may include, but are not limited to:
- Personal Loans: Unsecured loans for individuals to cover personal expenses.
- Business Loans**: Loans to businesses for operational or capital needs. - Installment Loan: Loans that are repaid through fixed periodic payments over a specified term.
Loan Application Process
- Eligibility Criteria: Define the criteria that potential borrowers must meet to qualify for a loan, including creditworthiness, income verification, and other relevant financial information.
- Application Submission: Borrowers must complete a loan application form and provide all necessary documentation to support their application.
- Review and Approval: The loan application will be reviewed by authorized personnel who will assess the borrower’s creditworthiness and repayment capacity. A decision will be communicated to the borrower within 5 business days.
Loan Agreement Terms
Once approved, a written loan agreement will be created that includes:
- Loan Amount: The principal amount being loaned to the borrower.
- Interest Rate: The rate at which interest will accrue on the loan.
- Repayment Schedule: Details on how and when payments are to be made, including the frequency of payments (monthly, quarterly, etc.) and the final maturity date.
- Fees and Charges: Outline any applicable fees, such as origination fees or late payment penalties.
- Default Terms: Clearly state the conditions under which the loan will be considered in default and the consequences that may follow.
Disbursement of Funds
Loan funds will be disbursed to the borrower’s designated bank account upon the mutual signing of the loan agreement and after any required conditions precedents have been met.
Loan Management and Monitoring
- Record Keeping: Maintain accurate records of all loan agreements, payment schedules, and borrower communications.
- Monitoring: Regularly monitor loan performance and repayment adherence. Send reminders to borrowers before payment due dates as stipulated in the loan agreement.
Default and Collection Procedures
In cases of non-payment or default:
- Notification: Provide written notice to the borrower regarding the delinquent payment and further actions that may be taken.
- Collection Actions: If needed, initiate collection actions in line with local laws and regulations, which may include involving third-party collection agencies.
Documentation and Confidentiality
All borrower information and loan agreements shall be treated as confidential and shall only be accessible to authorized personnel involved in the loan management process, in compliance with applicable data protection laws.
Review of the Policy
This Loan Agreement Policy shall be reviewed at least annually to ensure its effectiveness, relevance, and compliance with applicable legislation and organizational objectives.
Non-Discrimination
The organization shall ensure that all loan offerings and decisions taken under this policy will be free from discrimination on the basis of race, religion, gender, age, or other protected characteristics.
This Loan Agreement Policy provides a comprehensive framework for managing the lending process effectively while protecting the interests of both the organization and the borrowers. Adhering to this policy ensures consistency, fairness, and transparency in all loan transactions.
